AtlasDays App Privacy

Last updated: July 20, 2026

Your travel history, photos, and location remain on your device or in your private Apple iCloud account. AtlasDays has no user accounts or data servers.

In short
  • Your travel data, photos, and location remain private and under your control.
  • Your travel history stays on your device. If you enable iCloud Sync, AtlasDays syncs it only through your private Apple iCloud account.
  • AtlasDays processes location and photo data on your device. It does not upload your photos or raw location data.
  • The app has no user accounts, advertising, or analytics. AtlasDays does not sell or share your app data.

1. Storage and iCloud

AtlasDays stores your trips, trackers, notes, settings, and local photo references on your device. It has no server that receives this data.

On new installations, iCloud Sync is on by default when iCloud is available. It syncs trips and trackers through your private Apple iCloud account. You can turn sync off in AtlasDays Settings.

Some preferences can also sync through Apple’s iCloud key-value storage. AtlasDays may process your device region, preferred languages, and App Store country locally to suggest a home country.

2. Location

Auto-Detect Trips is optional. It uses Apple’s significant-change location service, not continuous GPS tracking. Country and US state checks happen on your device. Raw coordinates are not stored or uploaded.

AtlasDays keeps a local log of detected places and dates to suggest trips and manage confirmed ongoing trips. It never creates a new trip from location data without your confirmation.

You can turn Auto-Detect off in AtlasDays or revoke location access in iOS Settings.

3. Photos

Photo Import is optional. AtlasDays reads dates, locations, and limited photo metadata on your device to suggest past trips. It adds nothing to your trip history until you confirm it.

AtlasDays does not upload your photos or raw photo locations. It may store local photo identifiers and cache small previews on your device. These previews are not uploaded or synced. Originals remain in Apple Photos.

Local photo identifiers may sync with a trip through iCloud, but the photo files do not. If you favorite or unfavorite a photo in AtlasDays, that action updates it in Apple Photos.

You can revoke Photos access in iOS Settings.

4. Notifications

Trip suggestions and tracker alerts are generated and scheduled on your device. Notification permission is optional.

5. Imports, Exports, and Sharing

Imports, exports, and PDF reports are processed locally. AtlasDays does not upload them. Data you deliberately share is handled by the app or service you choose.

6. Purchases and Support

Apple handles purchases and subscriptions. AtlasDays receives your entitlement status, not your payment details.

Sending feedback is optional. AtlasDays prepares an editable email that may include basic app, device, settings, permission, and usage information to help diagnose problems. You can review or remove this information before sending. If you send the email, its contents are used only for support and kept only as long as reasonably needed. You may request deletion by contacting support.

7. Analytics and Advertising

AtlasDays has no advertising or third-party analytics SDKs and does not track your app use.

8. Your Controls

You can delete travel data in Settings, revoke permissions in iOS Settings, and turn off iCloud Sync. Deleting app data does not cancel Apple purchases or subscriptions.

9. More Information

If the app’s privacy model changes, this page and its update date will change.

10. Contact
