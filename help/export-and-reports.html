Export and Reports

Export your AtlasDays trip record for backup, review, transfer, or paperwork, and know what to clean up before you share it.

What This Page Helps You Do

Use this page when you need a copy of your trip data outside AtlasDays, want a shareable report, or want to check what the app will include before you send it anywhere.

By the end of this page, you should know:

  • when to use CSV versus PDF
  • what each export format actually contains
  • what to verify before you export a record you plan to rely on

Export reflects the trip record you have now. It does not repair bad dates, resolve overlaps, or turn approximate history into exact proof. Clean the record first, then export it.

When Export Is Useful

  • use CSV when you want a spreadsheet-friendly copy of your trip record for backup, transfer, or your own review
  • use PDF when you want a cleaner report for human review, records, or compliance paperwork
  • export before major cleanup if you want a checkpoint, and after cleanup if you want a record you can rely on

In the current app, both CSV export and PDF export require AtlasDays Pro.

Where Export Lives in the App

Go to Settings > Export. AtlasDays then lets you choose the export period and preview either CSV or PDF before you share the file.

If the selected period contains no trips, the export buttons stay disabled.

Choose the Right Export Scope First

Export is period-based. The screen supports:

  • All Time
  • Year, including a custom fiscal-year-style period
  • Range, using presets like 365, 180, 90, 30, or 7 days, or a custom start and end date

If you export a limited year or range, AtlasDays only includes trips it can place in that period. Unknown-date trips only appear in All Time exports because they have no dates to place in a filtered period.

CSV Export: What It Is For

CSV export is the trip-data format. It is best when you want to inspect the record in a spreadsheet, keep a portable copy, or re-import the same trip record later.

The exported CSV contains these columns:

  • Country
  • Start Date
  • End Date
  • Days
  • Transit
  • Notes

What those fields mean in practice:

  • Country is exported as a human-readable place name.
  • Exact-date trips export exact dates in yyyy-mm-dd.
  • Year-only trips export years in the Start Date and End Date columns.
  • Unknown-date trips export with blank date fields.
  • Ongoing trips export with a blank End Date.
  • Transit is exported as its own flag.
  • Notes export exactly as stored on the trip.

The CSV export is trip data only. It does not export app settings, home-country configuration, or tracker definitions.

How CSV Export Relates to Import

Import brings trip data into AtlasDays. Export takes your current AtlasDays trip record out.

AtlasDays can import its own exported CSV later because the importer accepts the extra Days column in the export format. If your goal is backup or transfer, export is the right tool. If your goal is rebuilding older history into the app, use CSV Import.

PDF Export: When It Fits

PDF export is the report-style output. It is better when you want a cleaner document for review, records, or paperwork rather than a spreadsheet.

The PDF report includes a trip table built from the currently selected export period. Depending on the options you choose, it can also include:

  • Tracker Summary
  • Traveler Information such as name, passport number, and nationality
  • Group by Country layout
  • App Branding
  • Disclaimer

If you turn on the disclaimer option, the PDF states that the report is based on user-entered data and is not an official travel record.

What to Verify Before You Export

  • Resolve any date conflicts in Timeline before you export a record you plan to trust.
  • Close ongoing trips if you need a fixed end date. Otherwise the current day span will keep changing.
  • Make sure trips that matter to a visa or residency threshold are stored as Exact Dates, not just Year or Unknown.
  • Review notes before sharing, because export includes them.
  • Check the selected period before exporting so you do not accidentally leave out relevant trips.

How Export Relates to Trackers and Dashboard

Export is built from your trip record. If the trip record is incomplete or approximate, the export will reflect that.

  • Tracker trust still depends on exact trips and clean conflict-free history.
  • CSV export is raw trip output, not a separate compliance calculation.
  • PDF Tracker Summary, if included, is generated from the trips in the current export period.
  • Dashboard is still the best place to inspect how the app is interpreting your record before you share it.
