Trip Modes and Import

How Exact Dates and Month & Year differ, when approximate history still helps, and how CSV import matches those same modes.

Why AtlasDays has two main trip modes

The app uses two primary entry modes because real travel history usually comes in two qualities: trips you know exactly, and trips you only remember approximately. AtlasDays lets you store both without forcing fake precision.

Exact Dates

Use Exact Dates when you know the actual arrival and departure dates, or when you want to pre-add a future trip. This is the only mode that supports ongoing trips and upcoming trips.

Exact-date trips are also what power precise tracker math, dashboard day totals, and the most reliable country-day calculations. If the number matters for a visa, tax, or border question, this is the mode you want whenever possible.

Month & Year

Use Month & Year when you know the rough window but not the exact days. You can store a single month, a month-to-month range, or clear both months and keep only the year, which effectively becomes a year-only trip.

This mode is designed for reconstructed history. It keeps the record honest without pretending you know the exact entry and exit days.

  • Approximate trips cannot be saved in the future.
  • Either both sides have a month, or neither side does.
  • Month & Year trips help with history and context, but they do not generate exact day counts.

Dates Unknown

Inside Month & Year mode, you can turn on Dates Unknown. That creates a country-only memory with no stored date fields. It is useful when you know you visited a place but cannot place it confidently in time yet.

Unknown-date trips appear at the bottom of the timeline in the No Date section. They preserve the fact of the visit, but they do not drive exact-day calculations.

Best practice: if a trip affects a real limit, keep it exact. Use Month & Year, year-only, or unknown entries to preserve incomplete history without contaminating the precise day math.

How the mode changes what you see

  • Ongoing and Upcoming sections: only exact-date trips can appear there.
  • Year sections: month-and-year and year-only trips are grouped into the relevant year.
  • No Date section: unknown-date trips go there.
  • Dashboard custom ranges: approximate trips only appear if your selected range fully contains the trip's possible window. AtlasDays avoids pretending a precise day count when the real date could have fallen outside the range.

Transit

Both Exact Dates and Month & Year trips can be marked as Transit from the extra options area. Transit entries stay in your history but are excluded from day counts.

CSV import and export

The CSV format mirrors the same four data shapes the app supports:

  • Exact dates: fill Start Date and End Date.
  • Ongoing exact trip: fill Start Date and leave End Date empty.
  • Month & Year: fill the month and year columns, leave exact-date columns empty.
  • Year only: fill only the year columns.
  • Unknown date: leave all date and month/year columns empty.

AtlasDays does not let you mix exact-date columns with approximate month/year columns in the same imported row. One row must use one precision model.

Import details

The CSV importer accepts both full country names and ISO country codes, and auto-detects whether your file uses commas or semicolons as the delimiter. Month columns accept names (January, Jan) or numbers (1, 01). The app shows a preview with per-row validation — unknown countries, invalid dates, and format problems are flagged before you confirm.

If you import trips that already exist in your history, duplicates are automatically skipped. You can also download a sample CSV template from the import screen that shows examples of all date precisions.

Overlap detection

If two exact-date trips share nights in the same or different countries, AtlasDays flags the overlap with a banner on the timeline. You can resolve overlaps by splitting one trip, trimming the end or start, merging both into one trip, or deciding later. This only applies to exact-date, non-transit trips.

The AI prompt helper

The app includes a built-in prompt you can copy into ChatGPT, Claude, or another assistant together with your raw travel notes. Its job is to convert messy source material into AtlasDays CSV faster, not to replace your review.
