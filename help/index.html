Before you trust a result

Fast checks

These are the highest-value checks to run before assuming the app math is wrong.

Check 1

Home Country or No fixed residence

Days Abroad depends on your Home Country choice in Settings. If that is wrong, the dashboard can look wrong even when the trip record is fine.

Check 2

Exact Dates vs Year or Unknown

Exact Dates feed exact counts. Year and Unknown preserve history but do not power exact tracker or rolling-window math. A same-day border crossing can still be a valid Exact Dates trip if you really entered and left on that day.

Check 3

ongoing trips

An Exact Dates trip with no end date is treated as ongoing, so AtlasDays uses today as the effective end date until you close it.

Check 4

Transit

Transit counts as 0 days. If you actually entered the country, even briefly, the trip should not be marked as Transit.

Check 5

Duplicates or overlaps

Imports and cleanup can create duplicate Exact Dates trips or overlapping trips. Duplicates repeat the same stay; overlaps are conflicting records that share dates. Both need review before you trust the result.

Check 6

Tracker scope, count mode, or permissions

If the issue is tracker-related, confirm the tracker countries and whether it is counting days or nights. From Dashboard, long-press the tracker card to switch between Count Nights and Count Days. If you expect alerts, confirm notification permission too. If the issue is export-related, confirm the selected export period first, because many "missing trip" reports are filtered exports rather than lost data.

Check 7

Tracker total vs country breakdown

In a multi-country tracker such as Schengen, the country rows in the detail view do not always add up to the tracker total. The rows show presence per country, but the tracker total counts each calendar day once across the whole tracked area. If one date touched three countries, each country row can show that day while the total still adds only 1 day.

Rule of thumb: if a trip affects a live visa limit, residency threshold, or near-term paperwork question, keep it in Exact Dates.

Email support

Support is best for one clear operational question, one tracker that looks wrong, one export that seems filtered incorrectly, or one privacy or iCloud sync behavior that does not match the docs.

Email AtlasDays support

For privacy, send only the minimum personal data needed to explain the issue. Avoid passport scans, full document uploads, or anything more sensitive than necessary.

Include these details

  • what you expected and what AtlasDays showed instead
  • the exact countries and dates involved, and whether the trips are Exact Dates, Year, Unknown, ongoing, Transit, duplicate, or overlapping
  • the selected tracker window, dashboard period, or export period if the issue depends on filtered output
  • whether Auto-Detect Trips, notification permission, or iCloud sync were enabled, and whether the app had been force-quit if the issue involved background behavior
  • screenshots of the relevant tracker, timeline entries, import preview, export screen, or permissions state
  • your iPhone model, iOS version, and AtlasDays app version if it looks like a bug
