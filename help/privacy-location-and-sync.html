Privacy, Location, and Sync

Understand AtlasDays' local-first privacy model, what permissions matter, and when optional iCloud sync is useful.

What This Page Helps You Do

Use this page when you are deciding whether to keep AtlasDays fully local, whether to enable country detection, or whether notifications and iCloud sync are worth turning on.

By the end of this page, you should know:

  • what AtlasDays keeps on your device by default
  • what location detection and notifications actually do in the app
  • whether iCloud sync fits your setup or should stay off

AtlasDays is local-first. There is no AtlasDays account, no sign-in, and no AtlasDays server copy of your trip history. Trips, trackers, and day calculations work on-device by default.

When to Use This Page

Come here during first-time setup, before turning on Auto-Detect Trips, after changing devices, or any time you want to understand what the app is doing with your data and permissions.

What AtlasDays Stores on Your Device

Your trip record and tracker data live in the app's local data store on your device. Manual trip entry, dashboard totals, tracker calculations, CSV import, and export all work without creating an AtlasDays account.

AtlasDays also keeps some device-level app state locally, such as pending trip suggestions, so the app can continue where you left off.

If you are signed into iCloud, AtlasDays can also mirror a small set of preferences such as home-country and appearance settings through Apple's iCloud key-value storage. That preference carry-over is separate from the full trip-and-tracker sync toggle.

How Country Detection Works

Auto-Detect Trips is optional. When you turn it on, AtlasDays uses iOS significant-change location monitoring to notice that you may have entered a different country and then checks which country that is.

AtlasDays does not silently create trips from location events. It creates a suggestion that you can review, edit, confirm, or dismiss. If the app is open, you see that suggestion in-app. If the app is in the background and notifications are allowed, you can receive a local notification.

  • AtlasDays uses significant-change location, not continuous GPS tracking.
  • Raw location history is not stored by AtlasDays and is not sent to an AtlasDays server.
  • The app avoids re-suggesting the same country until you leave and enter a different one.
  • You can leave auto-detect off and log everything manually in Trip Modes and Record Quality.

Which Permissions Matter

  • Location matters only if you want automatic country-detection suggestions. Manual entry, dashboard review, import, and export do not require it.
  • Notifications matter if you want background trip-suggestion alerts or tracker alerts. They are not required for manual logging.
  • For the most reliable border detection, allow location and choose Always Allow if iOS offers that upgrade.

If iOS location or notification permission is denied later, AtlasDays mirrors that and turns the related in-app toggle off rather than pretending the feature is still active.

What Notifications Are For

AtlasDays uses local notifications for two jobs:

  • Trip suggestions when the app detects that you may have entered a new country while AtlasDays is in the background
  • Tracker alerts such as approaching a visa or residency threshold

These alerts are scheduled on your device. They are not routed through an AtlasDays server.

When to Use iCloud Sync and When to Leave It Off

Turn on iCloud Sync if you want your trips and trackers available across multiple Apple devices or you want your private Apple iCloud account to hold the sync-backed copy of that data.

Leave it off if you use one device only, prefer to keep the full trip record local to that device, or would rather rely on manual file exports for backup and transfer.

This page is the privacy and permissions overview. For the exact sync behavior, restart expectations, and device-change details, use iCloud Sync and Restore.
