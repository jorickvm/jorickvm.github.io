FAQ

How does day counting work?

AtlasDays counts both the arrival day and the departure day. Open trips use today as the effective end date. For country totals and trackers, overlapping exact-date days are deduplicated so the same day is not counted twice.

What are trackers?

Trackers are counting rules applied to selected countries in your trip history. Use them for limits and goals like Schengen 90/180, residency thresholds, visa limits, or personal travel targets.

What tracker types are available?

AtlasDays supports three tracker window types: Rolling window, Per stay, and Yearly. Yearly trackers can use a normal calendar year or a custom fiscal-year start.

How does location detection work?

If you enable auto-detect, AtlasDays uses iOS significant location updates plus Apple geocoding to detect the current country and create a suggestion instead of automatically adding a trip. You can confirm, edit, or dismiss it, and you can turn the feature off at any time in Settings.

Can I import or export my data?

Yes. AtlasDays supports CSV import and export from Settings. You can back up your trips, clean them up in a spreadsheet, or import existing travel history into the app.

What does Transit mean?

If a stop was transit only, you can mark it as Transit. Transit entries stay in your history and may appear separately on the map, but they do not count toward day totals, trackers, or visited-place counts.

How are visited countries counted?

AtlasDays supports three counting modes: UN 195, UN 195+2, and All Places. UN 195 counts 193 UN member states plus Vatican City and Palestine. UN 195+2 adds Kosovo and Taiwan. All Places also includes territories and dependencies.

What about incomplete dates?

AtlasDays supports exact-date trips, year-only trips, and unknown-date visits. Year-only and unknown entries preserve your history without inventing fake precision, but they do not feed exact day totals or tracker calculations.

Do I need an account or iCloud?

No account is required. AtlasDays works locally by default. iCloud sync is optional and uses your personal Apple iCloud account.
