Product

How AtlasDays works

Three views built around the same trip record. Log once, review everywhere.

Interactive world map

Visited countries in your accent color, transit countries in a separate color, micro-nations as dot markers. Tap any country to see trips and days. Share map cards directly.

Dashboard with live stats

Countries visited, trips taken, days abroad, and continents — each tappable for the full breakdown. Switch between year view, all-time, or custom date ranges.

Tracker cards with alerts

Rolling windows, per-stay limits, yearly counting, and custom fiscal years. Progress bars shift color as you approach a limit. Notifications fire at key milestones.

Flexible trip logging

Exact dates for trips that matter to a count. Year-only or unknown entries for older history. Mark airport layovers as transit. Add notes, flag ongoing trips, log future plans.

CSV import and AI helper

Import from spreadsheets, export your full history. A built-in prompt turns booking emails or passport stamps into import-ready CSV. The preview catches errors before anything is saved.

Auto-detect country arrivals

Optional low-power country detection suggests trips as you cross borders. One suggestion per crossing, no GPS tracking, no data sent anywhere.

In the app

Numbers you can act on, not just look at

Every screen is designed so you can answer a real question — how many days left, which countries count, what does my record look like — without digging through formulas.

Timeline

Your trips, grouped and searchable

The timeline is the source of truth. Trips stay grouped by year, ongoing and upcoming trips surface first, and day totals stay visible on every card instead of being buried in detail screens.

  • Search across countries, notes, and years
  • Swipe to delete with a few seconds to undo
  • Overlap detection when trips share nights

Map

A map that does more than decorate

Visited countries appear in color, transit countries stay separate, and every country is tappable. The map changes with the selected period so you can see your history evolve year by year instead of staring at one static total.

  • Mini map on the dashboard and a full-screen interactive view
  • Country detail sheets with trip lists and day totals
  • Shareable cards with your map, stats, and flags

Trackers

Trackers built for different rules

AtlasDays is not locked to one formula. The same trip record can power Schengen tracking, per-stay visa limits, yearly residence counts, and custom fiscal-year thresholds.

  • Preset trackers for Schengen, residence, visa limits, and travel goals
  • Rolling windows, per-stay counting, calendar years, and fiscal years
  • Warnings as you approach a limit instead of manual recounting

Trip Modes

Messy history still works

Older travel rarely comes in clean, exact dates. AtlasDays lets you choose the precision level that matches what you actually know, so reconstruction is possible without pretending every trip is perfect.

  • Exact Dates for precise day counting
  • Year for reconstructed history
  • Unknown support when a trip is real but incomplete

Import

Bring in old travel without wrecking the record

CSV import is built around review, not blind upload. You map columns, inspect the preview, catch bad rows early, and only save once the result looks right.

  • Import from spreadsheets and existing logs
  • Preview catches issues before anything is saved
  • Pairs naturally with the app's cleanup and export tools

FAQ

Common questions

The things most people want to know before deciding whether AtlasDays is for them.

Can it track Schengen 90/180 and other limits?

Yes. AtlasDays supports rolling windows, per-stay limits, calendar-year counting, and custom trackers.

Can I add older trips with incomplete dates?

Yes. AtlasDays supports exact trips, year-only entries, and unknown-date visits.

Does it store my precise location?

No. Country detection is optional and designed around country suggestions, not continuous location surveillance.

Can I import travel history I already have?

Yes. You can import CSV, export your data, and use the built-in template and import guidance to clean up existing records.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Your trips, trackers, and map stay available on-device without an internet connection.

Is this legal or tax advice?

No. AtlasDays helps you keep records and count days. Interpretation of immigration or tax rules is still your responsibility.

How much will AtlasDays cost?

Pricing details will be announced closer to launch.

Is there an Android version?

No Android version is planned yet. AtlasDays is currently focused on iPhone and iPad.
