AtlasDays Privacy

Last updated: March 24, 2026

AtlasDays is a private travel-history app for iPhone and iPad. This page explains two separate things: how the app handles your trip data, and how the website handles optional waitlist emails if you submit the signup form.

TL;DR

The AtlasDays app itself does not require an account and does not keep a server-side copy of your travel history.

If you join the website waitlist, we collect the email address you submit so we can send launch updates.

Optional iCloud sync stores your app data in your own Apple iCloud account, not on AtlasDays servers.

1. The App Does Not Require an Account

You do not need to create an account, sign in, or provide personal information to use the AtlasDays app. The app is designed to work locally on your device.

2. App Data Stays on Your Device

Your trip data is stored on your device using Apple's local data storage frameworks. AtlasDays does not upload your travel history, day counts, trip notes, or tracker data to an AtlasDays-operated server.

If you enable iCloud sync, your data may sync across your own devices through your personal iCloud account. That sync is handled by Apple and governed by Apple's privacy policies.

3. Website and Waitlist Emails

The website includes an optional waitlist form. If you submit that form, we collect the email address you provide so we can send AtlasDays launch updates and occasional product news.

The form is processed through Formspree, which acts as a form-processing service for the website. We do not ask for more information than your email address on the waitlist form.

  • Submitting the waitlist form is optional.
  • The waitlist is separate from the app itself.
  • We do not sell waitlist data.
  • You can contact us to ask for your waitlist email to be removed.

4. Location Detection

AtlasDays can optionally request location access to detect country changes and suggest new trips. This feature is opt-in.

  • The app is designed around country detection, not continuous location tracking.
  • Raw coordinates are not stored as part of your trip history.
  • AtlasDays does not transmit your location history to an AtlasDays server.
  • You can revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings.

5. Notifications

AtlasDays can send local notifications for trip suggestions and tracker alerts. These are generated on-device.

6. CSV Import and Export

The app can import and export CSV files. Those files are handled locally through the Apple share sheet and file picker. AtlasDays does not upload the contents of your CSV files to an AtlasDays server.

7. Analytics and Advertising

The app does not include advertising SDKs. The website also does not run third-party analytics scripts at this time.

8. Changes to This Page

If the AtlasDays privacy model or website data handling changes, this page will be updated and the date above will change.

9. Contact

If you have privacy questions or want your waitlist email removed, contact atlasdays [at] pm.me.
