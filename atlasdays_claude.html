FAQ

Common questions

Does AtlasDays store my location?
No. AtlasDays uses low-power location monitoring only to detect country changes. Your precise location is never stored or transmitted — only the country code is kept locally on your device.
Can I track Schengen 90/180?
Yes. AtlasDays includes a built-in preset for the Schengen 90/180 rolling window rule, covering all 29 Schengen countries. You can see exactly how many days remain in your current window.
Can I add trips with approximate dates?
Yes. You can log trips with exact dates, month and year, or year only. This is useful for building up older travel history when you don't remember precise dates.
Does AtlasDays give legal or tax advice?
No. AtlasDays is a personal travel tracking tool. It helps you count days and stay organized, but it is not a substitute for professional legal, tax, or immigration advice.
Can I import my past travel history?
Yes. You can import trips from a CSV file. AtlasDays provides a template to help you format your data correctly for bulk import.
Is my data synced anywhere?
Only if you choose to enable iCloud sync. When enabled, your data syncs through your personal iCloud account across your Apple devices. No other servers are involved.
