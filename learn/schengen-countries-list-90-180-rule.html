Schengen Countries List for the 90/180 Rule

The Schengen Area members that count together under the short-stay 90/180 rule.

Last verified: March 2026

Short answer

The Schengen 90/180 rule applies across the 29 countries in the Schengen Area. For short stays, your days are pooled across all of them, so time in France, Italy, and Spain all counts toward the same limit.

That is why the country list matters: the rule is shared by the whole area, not reset every time you cross a border.

Important distinction: not every EU country is in Schengen. Ireland is in the EU but not in Schengen. Cyprus is also an EU country, but it is not yet part of the Schengen short-stay system. The UK is outside Schengen entirely.

Why this list matters

If a country is in Schengen, its days count toward the same 90-day limit as every other Schengen country. A two-week trip split between Amsterdam, Berlin, and Barcelona still uses 14 Schengen days.

If a country is not in Schengen, its stay rules are separate. That is why travelers often need to distinguish between EU membership and Schengen membership before they plan a trip.

How to think about it in practice

  1. Check whether your destination is inside the Schengen Area, not just the EU.
  2. Count all days spent inside any Schengen country together.
  3. Use the 180-day rolling lookback when planning future entry dates.
  4. Do not assume that crossing a border resets your allowance.

That simple habit prevents the most common mistake: treating the Schengen area like a collection of separate country counters when it is actually one shared pool.
