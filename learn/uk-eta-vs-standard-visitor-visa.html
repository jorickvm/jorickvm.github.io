UK ETA vs Standard Visitor Visa: What's the Difference?

If you're visiting the UK, the right answer depends on your nationality, your reason for travel, and whether you need a visa or ETA.

Last verified: March 2026

The short answer

An ETA is travel permission for eligible nationalities. A Standard Visitor visa is a visa. They are not the same thing, and neither one automatically guarantees entry at the border.

In practice, many visitors will use an ETA if they are eligible, while others need a Standard Visitor visa instead. The correct route depends on your passport and the reason you are coming to the UK.

Do not treat an ETA as automatic entry. GOV.UK says an ETA does not guarantee entry to the UK. Border officers still decide whether you can enter when you arrive.

ETA vs Standard Visitor visa

Question ETA Standard Visitor visa
What is it? Permission to travel to the UK for eligible nationalities. A visa for visitors who need to apply for a visitor route before travel.
Who uses it? People whose nationality is eligible for an ETA. People who are not eligible for ETA, or whose circumstances require a visa route.
How long can you stay? Usually up to 6 months for permitted visitor activities. Usually up to 6 months, depending on the visitor type and endorsement.
Does it guarantee entry? No. No.

When an ETA is usually the right route

Use the ETA path if your nationality is eligible and you are coming for a permitted short visit, such as tourism, visiting friends or family, business travel, or short study. GOV.UK also notes that many visitors from Europe, the USA, Australia, Canada, and certain other countries usually need an ETA rather than a visa.

If you already know you are eligible, the ETA route is simpler than a visa application. But you still need to check the official eligibility tool before relying on it.

When you may need a visa instead

Some people need a Standard Visitor visa instead of an ETA. The most obvious cases are people whose nationality is not ETA-eligible and people whose trip purpose falls outside the ETA route.

GOV.UK also points out that if you have a criminal record or were previously refused entry to the UK, you may want to apply for a Standard Visitor visa instead. That is a high-level rule of thumb, not a substitute for checking the official route checker.

The Standard Visitor rules also cover cases where people need a visitor visa because they are applying from a visa-national country, or because their travel reason needs a specific visitor category rather than the ETA path.

What the Standard Visitor route covers

The Standard Visitor route is for temporary visits, usually up to 6 months, for activities such as tourism, visiting family, business trips, and short courses. That route is broader than the ETA flow, because it is the visitor visa category the immigration rules use for those who need a visa to visit.

How to check the right answer

  1. Start with the official GOV.UK visa checker.
  2. Confirm whether your nationality can use an ETA.
  3. Check whether your reason for travel fits the visitor route you want.
  4. Read the Standard Visitor rules if you are unsure how long you can stay or what activities are allowed.
